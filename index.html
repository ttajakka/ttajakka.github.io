Research

My research focuses on algebraic geometry. In particular, I'm interested in moduli spaces of sheaves and complexes on varieties, as well as moduli of twisted sheaves and principal bundles.

My postdoctoral mentor is Sofia Tirabassi. I did my PhD at the University of Washington, where my advisor was Jarod Alper. My current collaborators include Stefan Reppen and Mihai Pavel.

  • Projectivity and effective global generation of determinantal line bundles on quiver moduli
    Joint with P. Belmans, C. Damiolini, H. Franzen, V. Hoskins, S. Makarova. We use stack-theoretic techniques to give a modern, GIT-free proof of the classical result that the moduli space of semistable representations of an acyclic quiver is a projective variety.
  • Uhlenbeck Compactification as a Bridgeland Moduli Space
    In this paper I prove that certain Bridgeland moduli spaces on surfaces are projective, and compare these moduli spaces to the Uhlenbeck compactification.
  • Projectivity of the moduli space of vector bundles on a curve
    Contribution to the Stacks Project Expository Collection (SPEC), with J. Alper, P. Belmans, D. Bragg, and J. Liang.
  • Projective moduli spaces of complexes of sheaves
    My PhD thesis.
